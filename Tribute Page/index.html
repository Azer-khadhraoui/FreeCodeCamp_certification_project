Marie Curie: Pioneer of Radioactivity

Marie Curie was a brilliant scientist whose work on radioactivity revolutionized science and medicine. She discovered the elements polonium and radium, and her research led to groundbreaking techniques for treating cancer.

Born in Poland, Curie moved to France to study at the Sorbonne and became the first female professor at the University of Paris. She faced numerous obstacles due to her gender but overcame them with courage and determination.
